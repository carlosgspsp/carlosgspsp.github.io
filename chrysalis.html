Chrysalis

As the lead programmer on the "Chrysalis" project, developed with the "Helheim Engine" in C++. I directed a team to create an immersive top-down shooter that showcased the engine's capabilities. I focused on refining player gunplay mechanics for responsive combat and implemented sophisticated AI behaviors using Finite State Machines (FSM) to enhance gameplay depth. This project highlighted my ability to work with complex systems and my commitment to delivering a high-quality gaming experience.

My Work

Gun Play

Designed and programmed core shooting mechanics, ensuring accurate aiming precision and realistic projectile physics for immersive combat. Developed weapon functionality, including firing modes (single shot, burst, shotgun) and recoil systems, to enhance realism.

Grenade

Developed a unique black hole grenade that pulls in nearby enemies. Once enemies are gathered, the grenade explodes, dealing area-of-effect damage, creating a tactical gameplay element that adds depth and strategy to combat.

Enemies Behaviour

Designed and implemented enemy AI using state machines, creating dynamic behaviors such as chasing, attacking, and retreating. Developed varied attack patterns to enhance gameplay challenge and immersion.

Pool Manager and Spawners

Designed and implemented a pool manager to optimize resource usage and improve performance during gameplay. Integrated the pool manager into enemy spawners, allowing for efficient spawning and management of enemies, reducing lag and ensuring smoother gameplay with large numbers of enemies.
